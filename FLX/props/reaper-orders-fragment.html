REAPER — Southern District — Field Directive
From: Cdr Kavan, REAPER Southern District
To: Watch Team 3, Facility Element
27 FEB — Directive 14

The facility is now in our area of responsibility. The government soldiers have left. This is our opportunity to establish control of this area before anyone returns.

Your watch team is assigned to observe and report. You are the eyes for the district. Follow these instructions:

1. Establish observation of the facility and all roads approaching it. The main north-south road and the junction to the north at the crossroads (grid MP 0602 1565) are the priority. Report any military movement immediately by radio.
2. Resupply will come from the supply cache through the main road from the south every two days. Do not interfere with the supply teams. Confirm receipt of each delivery by radio.
3. I will send orders and updates by courier using the western forest road. Couriers will travel on foot. They will use the recognition phrase "spring water" — challenge them and confirm before accepting any message.
4. Do not engage military forces unless you are directly attacked. Your task is to observe and report, not to fight. If military forces approach the facility in strength, withdraw immediately to the crossroads junction and send a report. Do not attempt to hold the facility.
5. If you are forced to withdraw, 2nd Company REAPER can reach the crossroads within four hours from the north. Hold the crossroads and maintain communication until reinforcements arrive. The crossroads controls all movement through this area — we must not lose it.

The facility and the roads around it are critical to REAPER operations. If we control this ground, we control movement through the entire southern district. Your reports are critical to our planning.

Maintain discipline. Stay concealed. Report everything.

For the People,
A. Kavan
Commander — REAPER Southern District
