# FLX After Action Review — Facilitator Guide

**CADRE USE ONLY**

**Operation SPRING STRIKE — Class 065 Culmination FLX**

---

This AAR has two parts. Part 1 gets candidates to assess their own performance — planning, execution, and internal TTPs. Part 2 collects structured feedback on the FTX itself so cadre can refine the exercise for next time. Cadre facilitates both parts.

Run the AAR at COP Seggel or at the recovery site Sunday morning. Candidates should still be in field uniforms with their equipment accessible — reference specific gear, maps, and products during the discussion. Budget 45–60 minutes total.

## Ground Rules (Read Aloud)

- This is a professional discussion, not a critique session. No rank pulling, no finger-pointing.
- Be specific — reference what actually happened, then talk about what should have happened and why.
- "I don't know" and "we didn't think about that" are honest answers. Say them.
- Cadre is taking notes on Part 2 to improve the FTX. Your feedback directly shapes what the next class gets.

---

## Part 1 — Candidate Operations Review (~30 min)

Lane-specific AARs already happened in the hot wash after each mission. This part focuses on the big-picture patterns that cut across the whole weekend — planning, leadership, communication, and how candidates adapted over time.

### Troop Leading Procedures

- Which of the 8 TLP steps did you consistently do well? Which ones fell off first when you were tired or rushed?
- How did your planning process change from Friday to Saturday? Did it get more efficient or did it just get shorter?
- **PL/PSG candidates:** How did you decide what to include in your order and what to leave to the SLs? Looking back, did you give them enough to work with?
- **SLs:** On Saturday, the PL/PSG planning cycle compressed your planning window. Did you recognize that as it was happening? What did you cut from your process to make the timeline work?
- Did anyone conduct a rehearsal that actually changed how you executed? Or did rehearsals become a checkbox?

### Leadership and Decision-Making

- When something went wrong during execution, how did you recover? Give a specific example.
- **PL/PSG candidates:** What was the hardest decision you made over the weekend? What information did you wish you had?
- **SLs:** Where did you have to make a call that wasn't covered in the order? How did you decide what to do?
- How did the transition from cadre as PL on Friday to candidate PL/PSG on Saturday feel? What was the biggest difference?
- Did fatigue change how you made decisions by Saturday afternoon compared to Friday morning?

### Communications and Tracking

- How well did comms work across the weekend? Where did they break down?
- Every Saturday mission involved leaving and returning to the COP. Did you plan recognition signals and re-entry procedures? Did it get better across the day or worse?
- Could you track where other elements were during operations? What would you need to do that better?
- How did information flow between the PL/PSG and SLs during execution — was it push, pull, or nothing?

### Overall Takeaways

- What is the one thing you personally got better at over the course of the weekend?
- What is the one thing you'd tell the next class to focus on before they come to the field?

## Part 2 — FTX Refinement Feedback (~20 min)

Transition clearly: *"Now we want your feedback on the exercise itself — the training plan, the orders, the training area, OPFOR, all of it. This is how we make the next FTX better. Be honest."*

### Thursday Preparation Training

- Which training blocks were most useful once you got to the field? Which ones didn't help?
- Was there a skill you needed in the field that we didn't train on Thursday?
- Did the training block format work (candidate-led presentations), or would a different format have been more effective?
- Was the time between receiving OPORD 001 Thursday afternoon and SP Friday morning enough for the PL to plan? Too much? Too little?

### Orders and Missions

- Were the OPORDs clear and easy to understand, or were there parts that confused you?
- Was the number of missions about right for the weekend, or were there too many / too few?
- Did the missions feel like they built on each other, or did they feel disconnected?
- Was the progression from Friday (cadre as PL) to Saturday (candidate PL/PSG) too big a jump, about right, or not enough of a change?
- Was the capstone (cache clearance) the right ending for the weekend? Did it feel like a culminating event?
- Was there a type of mission you wanted to do but didn't get to?

### Training Area and Facilities

- How was the COP site? Did the buildings and layout support the training, or were there problems?
- Were the objective areas (ambush site, CONEX city, OP location) realistic and usable?
- Was the terrain challenging enough? Too easy to navigate? Any safety concerns?
- Did the distances between the COP and the objectives work — too far, too close, about right?

### OPFOR

- Did the OPFOR feel realistic? Too easy? Too hard?
- Was OPFOR consistent across lanes, or did it feel random?
- Was the capstone OPFOR element large enough to make the clearance challenging?
- Any suggestions for making OPFOR more effective or realistic?

### Sustainment and Admin

- How was the food/water situation across the weekend? Did you have enough?
- Was the equipment adequate? Anything you needed that you didn't have?
- How was the sleep plan? Did you get enough rest to function, or were you dangerously degraded?
- The GSA van delivery — did the timing work? Any issues with the resupply process?
- No blank ammo / simulators — how much did that affect the training value? Would blanks have made a meaningful difference?

### Cadre Support

- Did you get enough feedback during and between lanes to know how you were doing?
- Was the feedback useful and specific, or was it too general?
- Was the balance between evaluation and coaching about right? Did you feel like cadre was grading you or developing you?
- Did you know what you were being evaluated on?

### Overall

- On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate the overall training value of the FLX?
- What was the single best part of the weekend?
- What was the single worst part?
- If you could change one thing about the FTX for the next class, what would it be?

## Cadre-Only Notes (Do Not Read to Candidates)

Capture the following during the AAR for the cadre hot wash afterward:

- **Candidate self-awareness:** Which candidates accurately assessed their own performance vs. which ones were off-base? This is a data point on self-development and learning agility.
- **Recurring planning failures:** If multiple candidates cite the same TLP step breaking down (e.g., everyone skipped rehearsals by Saturday), that's a training design issue, not just an individual weakness.
- **Time management pattern:** Track whether the PL/PSG planning time compression was a consistent problem. If SLs are consistently getting squeezed, future iterations may need to build in more structured planning windows or coach PL/PSG candidates on time management earlier.
- **FTX design changes:** Capture specific, actionable refinements for next iteration. Separate "nice to have" from "must fix."
- **Battle tracking gap:** This was identified as a known gap. Listen for whether candidates noticed the absence or whether it wasn't on their radar. Informs how to introduce it next time.
