### April Training Plan - Ability Grouping Approach

**Training Schedule**:  

- Friday: CR9 and 10 training areas, 7:30-15:30 (must vacate by 15:30)
- Saturday: PHA (limits training availability)
- Sunday: Closeout
- Realistic training time: approximately 1.5 days

**Two-Group Structure**:     

- **TLP-Focused Group**: Candidates needing more repetitions on Troop Leading Procedures, op order briefings, and planning fundamentals. Will stop training before crossing the Line of Departure to maximize iteration cycles
- **Tactical Execution Group**: Candidates comfortable with TLPs who want to refine tactical execution and movement. Will complete full lane execution including movement and assault
- Self-selection encouraged - candidates can switch groups if they realize they need different training

**Thursday/Friday Split**: 

- Thursday: All candidates train together in LTA using unified approach
- Friday: Split into two ability groups for targeted training

**Group Participation**: Approximately 40% of class (10 candidates) initially indicated interest in tactical execution group 

### Key FTX Feedback Themes

**Squad Rotation Issues**:    

- Same squads remained in security positions throughout multiple iterations, limiting learning opportunities for some candidates
- Second squad stayed as assault squad across missions after initial planning decisions
- Third squad maintained security role throughout
- Recommendation: Rotate squad assignments between missions or renumber squads to change roles while maintaining taskings

**OPFOR Concerns**:     

- Some candidates felt they missed training value by serving as OPFOR rather than participating in planning/briefing
- Tradeoff identified: OPFOR sees tactical execution but misses TLP process and peer briefings
- Consensus that hearing op order briefs and participating in planning has more value for Phase 3 success than observing execution
- Phase 3 focus is on planning/briefing, not execution (execution is 5-10% of evaluation)

**Briefing Quality**:   

- Op order briefings during FTX were difficult to follow in some cases
- Vision was for squad leader-level briefing practice similar to Phase 3
- Execution didn't match intended standard due to resource constraints and planning decisions

**Leadership Rotation Success**:   

- Unplanned leadership rotation worked well - candidates progressed through roles (Joe → Team Leader → Squad Leader → PSG → PL)
- Allowed candidates to observe leaders above them, then step into those roles
- Previous leaders were removed from planning area to prevent over-influencing next leader
- Should be intentionally planned in future iterations

**Work/Rest Plan Implementation**:   

- Leadership did well managing rest plan when returning from missions
- Issue: Assault teams sometimes pulled tower security immediately after missions without adequate rest
- Need to better incorporate which squads were "heaviest worker" in previous mission when assigning next tasks

### Phase 3 Preparation Guidance

**What Resources to Request**:   

- Artillery or mortar support
- Grenades
- Colored smoke
- Notional security
- Satellite imagery
- Map (always ask)
- Depends on what instructor has available - if you see equipment on their kit, request it
- Must provide good justification for requests

**Critical Briefing Requirements**:   

- Must orient people to the map correctly
- Sand table must be positioned facing north (north arrow must align with actual north)
- Failure to orient sand table correctly can result in automatic failure
- Hit all main briefing criteria points - some carry more weight than others

**Philosophy on Tactics**:   

- Phase 3 does not evaluate tactical soundness - focus is on planning process
- Could theoretically use "Army Revolution style line formation" and pass if properly planned
- Most candidates are not going infantry - will learn branch-specific tactics at BOLC
- Critical thinking and decisiveness matter more than perfect tactical execution

### Cultural Expectations

**Feedback Environment**:    

- Professional discussion with no rank-pulling
- Everyone needs thick skin to accept constructive feedback
- Applies to cadre as well - must be able to receive feedback
- Be specific and reference what actually happened
- "I don't know" and "we didn't think about that" are valid answers
- Not trick questions - feedback directly shapes next class training
- If unable to take feedback, wrong profession

**Critical Thinking Emphasis**:    

- Course is about developing critical thinking, not just following orders
- Officers must think critically beyond what NCOs provide
- Goal is decisive leaders who will make decisions and learn from both success and failure
- Candidates encouraged to make requests - worst answer is "no"
- Should question confusing guidance and ask for clarification

**Learning from Failure**: 

- Failing is a form of success - shows what needs improvement
- Focus less on getting everything right, more on learning and adapting
- Program aims to produce decisive leaders, not perfect soldiers
