## 1. SITUATION

### a. Area of Interest
Clay County, Florida, in the vicinity of Camp Blanding Joint Training Center. 1st Platoon occupies COP Seggel (MP 0610 1550). The structure complex and CONEX yard south of COP Seggel along Bradenton Avenue (vicinity MP 0608 1530) is the target area. Routes south of the complex connect to REAPER-controlled areas beyond AO HERRERA.

### b. Area of Operations

**1. Terrain.** The area south of COP Seggel transitions from the COP perimeter into dense woodlines along Bradenton Avenue, opening into a partially cleared area containing multiple structures, shipping containers, and storage buildings in the vicinity of MP 0608 1530. The complex is bounded by dense woodlines on three sides (east, south, west) with the Bradenton Avenue corridor providing the primary approach from the north. Fields of fire within the complex are limited by the structures themselves — close-quarters engagements between buildings with minimal standoff distance. The woodlines surrounding the complex restrict observation of the approaches and provide concealed positions for both security elements and any REAPER early warning positions. An improved road connects COP Seggel to the complex along Bradenton Avenue, offering a direct but exposed approach.

   - **Key Terrain:** The structure complex itself (vicinity MP 0608 1530) is the decisive terrain — it contains the weapons and materiel cache. The woodline edge north of the complex provides concealed positions for SBF and assault staging. The Bradenton Avenue corridor between COP Seggel and the complex is a danger area — exposed and likely under observation. The southern tree line provides REAPER escape routes out of the complex toward positions south of the AO. Individual structures within the complex create multiple rooms and corners that must be systematically cleared.

### c. Enemy Forces. REAPER: Resistance Elements of Atropia's People's Revolutionary Front
Documents captured during the ambush on the REAPER resupply element (OPORD 003) reveal a weapons and materiel cache in the structure complex south of COP Seggel (vicinity MP 0608 1530). The cache includes ammunition, IED-making materials, communications equipment, and food stores. The complex serves as a REAPER logistics hub and staging area. REAPER has been significantly degraded by platoon operations over the past 24 hours but retains a security presence at the cache site.

**1. Composition.** Estimated squad-sized REAPER element (6-10 personnel) with small arms and communications equipment providing security for the cache. REAPER fighters are dispersed among multiple structures within the complex and are familiar with the layout. Additional REAPER elements south of the AO may respond within 30-60 minutes if alerted.

**2. Disposition.** REAPER security is distributed across the structure complex rather than concentrated in a single position. Expect sentries on the northern and southern approaches, with the main body sheltering in the larger structures. REAPER has had time to establish hasty fighting positions within the buildings but has not fortified the complex. Observation posts may be positioned in the woodlines north of the complex to provide early warning along the Bradenton Avenue corridor.

**3. Most Probable Course of Action (MPCOA).** REAPER detects the friendly approach, alerts the security element, and fights a short delaying action from the northern structures while the main body attempts to withdraw south through the woodlines with high-value items (radios, documents, IED materials). REAPER avoids decisive engagement to preserve remaining combat power.

**4. Most Dangerous Course of Action (MDCOA).** REAPER defends the complex in depth, fighting from building to building with the intent to inflict maximum casualties and delay the clearance long enough for a reinforcing element to arrive from the south. REAPER employs hasty obstacles (overturned furniture, blocked doorways) and interlocking fields of fire between structures to slow the assault.

## 3. EXECUTION

### a. Commander's Intent

**Purpose:** Destroy REAPER's remaining logistics infrastructure to prevent reconstitution of enemy combat capability in AO HERRERA.

**Key Tasks:** (1) Isolate the structure complex to prevent REAPER withdrawal or reinforcement. (2) Clear all structures within the complex systematically. (3) Locate, inventory, and destroy the weapons and materiel cache. (4) Consolidate on the objective and return to COP Seggel.

**End State:** All structures cleared, REAPER security element destroyed or captured, cache destroyed, platoon consolidated at COP Seggel.

### b. Concept of Operations
1st Platoon departs COP Seggel and moves south along covered routes to the structure complex at OBJ IRON. The platoon establishes an ORP, conducts a leader's reconnaissance, and executes a deliberate clearance of the complex. Elements isolate the objective to prevent REAPER escape or reinforcement while the assault element clears structures systematically. Upon clearance, the platoon conducts site exploitation — inventory and destroy all cache materials of no intelligence value, collect documents and electronics for intelligence. Consolidate on the objective, conduct ACE/LACE, and return to COP Seggel. Time on the objective is extended compared to previous operations — thorough clearance takes priority over speed, but the platoon must complete the operation before EENT (1855). Indirect fire support available through company on request.

### c. Scheme of Fires
1st Platoon has priority of indirect fires. Indirect fire support available through company on request.

### d. Tasks to Subordinate Units

**1. 1st Platoon (Main Effort — Decisive Operation):**

- **TASK:** Depart COP Seggel and move to an ORP vicinity OBJ IRON. Conduct a leader's reconnaissance of the structure complex. Isolate the objective to prevent REAPER escape or reinforcement. Clear all structures within the complex systematically. Locate and destroy the weapons and materiel cache. Collect all documents, electronics, and materials of intelligence value. Consolidate on the objective and return to COP Seggel.
- **PURPOSE:** Eliminate REAPER's logistics hub to deny enemy reconstitution and complete the clearing of AO HERRERA.

All squads participate. COP security is notional during this operation.

### e. Coordinating Instructions

**1. Rules of Engagement (ROE):**
   1. Engage confirmed enemy combatants posing a threat.
   2. Positively identify targets before firing.
   3. Clear every room and structure — do not bypass.
   4. Detain and search any unarmed personnel found in the complex.
   5. Challenge unknown personnel using challenge and password.

**2. Priority Intelligence Requirements (PIR):**
   1. REAPER strength and disposition within the structure complex.
   2. Location of the primary cache within the complex.
   3. REAPER reinforcement response from positions south of the AO.
   4. Documents, maps, or electronics indicating REAPER command structure or future operations.

**3. Commander's Critical Information Requirements (CCIR):**
   1. REAPER element larger than expected (more than 12 personnel)
   2. REAPER reinforcement approaching from the south
   3. Friendly casualties during the clearance
   4. IEDs or booby traps encountered in the structures
   5. Civilian presence in the complex

**4. Reporting:**
   - **Report to higher when departing COP Seggel.**
   - **Set Report:** When ORP is established and leader's recon is complete.
   - **Isolation Report:** When blocking/security positions are set.
   - **SALUTE Report:** Upon contact.
   - **Progress Reports:** As each structure/building is cleared.
   - **Objective Secure Report:** When all structures are cleared and cache is located.
   - **ACE/LACE Report:** Upon consolidation on the objective.

**5. Re-entry to COP Seggel:** Departing and returning elements will coordinate re-entry to COP Seggel using the running password and pre-coordinated recognition signals. The PL must establish link-up procedures with COP Seggel prior to departure and report when approaching the COP perimeter.

**6.** This OPORD is effective upon receipt.
